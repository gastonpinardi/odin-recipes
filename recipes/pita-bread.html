Pita Bread

Pita bread is a round, leavened flat bread

Pita bread is a soft, round, and flatbread that is commonly consumed in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines. It is typically made from a mixture of flour, water, yeast, and salt, which is kneaded into a dough and then allowed to rise before baking. During the baking process, the dough puffs up and creates a pocket in the center of the bread, which can be filled with a variety of ingredients.

Pita bread is a versatile bread that can be enjoyed in many ways. It can be served as a side dish to accompany a meal, or used as a base for sandwiches, wraps, or pizzas. The pocket in the center of the bread makes it easy to stuff with fillings like falafel, hummus, vegetables, or meats. Pita bread is a popular food item around the world due to its soft texture, mild flavor, and ability to be easily filled and eaten on-the-go.

Ingredients:

  • Flour
  • Water
  • Yeast
  • Salt
  • Olive oil (Optional for flavor)

Steps:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 3 cups of flour, 1 tsp of salt, and 1 packet of instant yeast.
  2. Add 1 cup of warm water and 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the mixing bowl.
  3. Stir the ingredients together until a shaggy dough forms.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  5. Grease a clean mixing bowl with olive oil and place the dough inside. Cover with a clean kitchen towel and let rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours or until it has doubled in size.
  6. Preheat the oven to 500°F (260°C) and place a baking sheet or pizza stone in the oven to preheat as well.
  7. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal pieces and roll them out into circles about 6 inches in diameter on a floured surface.
  8. Place the circles on the preheated baking sheet or pizza stone and bake for 5-7 minutes or until the bread puffs up and begins to brown on the edges.
  9. Remove the pita bread from the oven and cover with a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm and moist.
  10. Repeat with the remaining circles of dough until all the pita bread is baked.
  11. Serve warm or at room temperature with your favorite dips, spreads, or fillings. Enjoy your homemade pita bread!
