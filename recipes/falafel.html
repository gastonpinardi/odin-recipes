Falafel

Falafel are deep-fried balls or patties made from chickpeas

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish that is commonly served as a street food. It is typically made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices such as cumin, coriander, and parsley. The mixture is then shaped into small balls or patties and deep-fried until crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

Falafel can be served in a variety of ways, such as in pita bread with vegetables and sauce, or as a topping for salads. It is often accompanied by hummus, tahini, or tzatziki sauce, and can be served with a side of pickles or olives. Falafel is a nutritious and filling dish that is high in protein and fiber, and is a popular choice among vegetarians and vegans. Its popularity has spread beyond the Middle East, and it is now enjoyed by people all over the world as a delicious and healthy meal.

Ingredients:

  • Chickpeas (or fava beans)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Fresh Parsley
  • Fresh Cilantro
  • Flour (Or breadcrumbs>
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Salt
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Baking soda
  • Oil

Steps:

  1. Soak 1 cup of dried chickpeas overnight in water with a pinch of baking soda.
  2. Drain and rinse the chickpeas, then add them to a food processor along with 1/2 cup of chopped onion, 4 cloves of garlic, 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro, 2 tablespoons of flour, 1 teaspoon of cumin, 1 teaspoon of coriander, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and a pinch of cayenne pepper.
  3. Pulse the mixture in the food processor until a coarse mixture forms. You may need to scrape down the sides of the bowl a few times to ensure that all of the ingredients are evenly mixed.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow it to firm up.
  5. Form the mixture into small balls or patties, about 1-2 inches in diameter.
  6. Heat about 2 inches of oil in a heavy-bottomed pot or deep fryer to 375°F.
  7. Fry the falafel in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per batch. Use a slotted spoon or spider strainer to remove the falafel from the oil and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  8. Serve the falafel in pita bread with chopped lettuce, tomatoes, cucumber, and tahini sauce or hummus, or as a topping for salads. Enjoy!
