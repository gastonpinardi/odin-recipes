Hummus

Hummus is a smooth and creamy puree

Hummus is a creamy, rich, and flavorful dip that originated in the Middle East. It is made from a blend of cooked chickpeas, tahini (a paste made from ground sesame seeds), lemon juice, garlic, and olive oil. The ingredients are typically combined in a food processor or blender until a smooth and creamy texture is achieved.

Hummus is a versatile dip that can be enjoyed in a variety of ways. It is commonly served as an appetizer with pita bread or vegetables for dipping. It can also be used as a spread for sandwiches or wraps, or as a topping for salads or roasted vegetables. Hummus is a healthy and nutritious food, as chickpeas are a good source of protein, fiber, and other essential nutrients. It is also naturally vegan and gluten-free, making it a great choice for people with dietary restrictions.

Ingredients:

  • Chickpeas
  • Tahini
  • Lemon juice
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Cumin (Optional)
  • Paprika (Optional)
  • Water (To adjust consistency)

Steps:

  1. Drain and rinse one 15-ounce can of chickpeas. You can also use cooked chickpeas if you prefer.
  2. Add the chickpeas to a food processor along with 1/4 cup of tahini, 1/4 cup of fresh lemon juice, 2 cloves of garlic (minced), 1/4 teaspoon of salt, and 1/4 teaspoon of cumin (optional).
  3. Turn on the food processor and blend the ingredients together until the chickpeas are finely ground and the mixture is smooth.
  4. While the food processor is running, slowly pour in 1/4 cup of olive oil until the mixture is creamy and well combined. You may need to stop and scrape down the sides of the food processor bowl a few times to ensure that all of the ingredients are evenly mixed.
  5. Taste the hummus and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add more salt, lemon juice, or cumin to taste.
  6. If the hummus is too thick, you can add a little bit of water to thin it out. Start with 1 tablespoon of water and blend again, repeating until you reach your desired consistency.
  7. Once the hummus is blended to your liking, transfer it to a serving bowl and drizzle with a little bit of olive oil and sprinkle with paprika (optional) for added flavor and presentation.
  8. Serve with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or use as a spread for sandwiches. Enjoy your homemade hummus!
